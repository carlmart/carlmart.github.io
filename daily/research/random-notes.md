
## Tradition Arak
 high-proof spirit (typically 40–60% ABV
```
  * One part Arak + 2 parts cold water + ice  - in that order
  * The Louche Effect - adding water cause clear Arak to turn milky white
    due to anise oils (anethole) becoming insoluable in diluted alcohol
  * adding ice before water can cause the anise oils to solidify into unsightly platelets.
  * Serving: traditionally served with mezze (small dishes) and consumed slowly over a meal
  * Ancient Roots and Phoenician Heritage
     lineage traces to the Phoenicians, who inhabited present-day Lebanon and were pioneers in viticulture.
```
